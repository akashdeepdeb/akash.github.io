Courses at UIUC

before Fall 2015

  • AP Calculus BC

    Course Review
    When I was in high-school, I didn't actually take this course since I was in a different curriculum. However, I self-studied the course from Coursera and the Barron's books. If you're reading this and are in high-school, I highly recommend taking it, as getting a '5' is not very hard and it allows you to get placed out of the strict Calc I & II requirements and jump directly to Calc III (Calc I, II can easily be self-studied, so you don't have to spend 1 or 2 semester(s) with them.)

    References
    Coursera: Calculus I by OSU (course) (Jim Fowler and Bart Snapp are both great professors!)
    Coursera: Calculus II by OSU (course) (The last AP Calc BC question is based on this.)
    OSU: MOOCulus I (pdf) (140 pages. Easy to get through with. This book is excellent!)
    OSU: MOOCulus II (pdf) (Just like the previous book; has exercises, great explanations; would definitely recommend it)
    Youtube: How to get a 5 in the AP Calculus BC test. (video) (I made this video a while back.)
